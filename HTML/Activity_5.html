ADOBO

Adobo is a classic Philippine meal. The marinade for adobo is made with pantry staples including white vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves.There are varius types of Adobo including Adobong manok, Adobong Baboy, and Adobong baka


Recipies

  1. 2 tbsp canola oil
  2. 6 cloves garlic crushed
  3. 1 pc onion, sliced
  4. 1 kilogram chicken cut ups
  5. 2 tbsp vinegar
  6. 1/4 cup soy sauce
  7. 1 cup water
  8. 2 pcs bay leaves
  9. 1 tsp whole black peppercorns, slightly crushed
  10. 2 pc Knorr chicken cubes
  11. 1 tsp brown sugar packed

Sinigang

Sinigang is a Filipino soup or stew characterized by its sour and savory taste. It is most often associated with tamarind or in Filipino it calles sampalok, although it can use other sour fruits and leaves as the souring agent. It is one of the more popular dishes in Filipino cuisine. The soup is usually accompanied by rice. In 2021, sinigang was rated as the world's best vegetable soup by TasteAtlas. There are many types of sinigang like Sinigang na manok, Sinigang na baboy, Sinigang na baka, etc.


Recipies

  1. 4¼ cups water
  2. 2 cups pork spare rib cut into chunks
  3. 1 pc red onion, quartered
  4. 2 pcs medium sized tomato, quartered
  5. 8 pcs sitaw, sliced into 2” pieces
  6. 250 g kangkong stalk and leaves
  7. 1 pc talong, sliced
  8. 2 pcs siling pangsigang
  9. 1 (20g) pack Knorr Sinigang sa Sampalok Mix Original
  10. Knorr Sinigang sa Sampalok Original

Pinakbet

Pinakbet also called Pakbet is a Filipino traditional vegetable dish. It consists of local vegetables like bitter melon, squash, sweet potatoes, eggplants, okra, and green beans combined with crispy pork. The original pinakbet recipe uses bagoong isda, an anchovy sauce sold in jars. Pinakbet originated in the region of Ilocos, but today it appears in many regional and seasonal varieties. The word pinakbet is derived from the Ilocano word pinakebbet, meaning shrunk or shrivelled.
